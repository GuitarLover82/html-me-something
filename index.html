Some Of My Favorite Concerts

Background Information

Music has always been the most important thing in my life. Most of my earliest memories are of myself listening to my father's vast record collection. As I got older, I discoverd "live music!" Music means differnt things to different people, and some people prefer different aspects of music than others. My personal favorite thing about music is seeing it performed live. I've been to too many concerts to count, but I would like to go over some of my favorite concerts and why they were my fovorites. The concerts I'll be discussing are:

  • Phish Big Cypress 1999
  • David Gilmour 2015
  • Roger Waters 2017
  • Brian Wilson 2016

Phish Big Cypress 1999

I first saw Phish in 1996, and, ever since then, they've been one of my favorite bands. There's a lot about Phish that sets them apart from every other band, but a few of the most important things are that no 2 shows are the same (every show has a differnt setlist of songs) and that everytime they play a song they play it differently. Becaouse of this, people travel all over the country to see their concerts, because each one is a different experience. You can watch a small viedo of Phish playing their song "The Divided Sky"

David Gilmour 2015

Another one of my favorite bands is Pink Floyd. Even though the band broke up in 1980, the members still tour the world perfoming their music which has impacted countless lives, including mine. David Gilmour was Pink Floyd's Guitar Player for most of their career. As a guitar player myself, when I found out that one of my all time favorite guitarists from one of my all time favorite bands was playing in Chicago, I decided I had to be there! I drove to Chicago with one of my dearest friends and long-time bandmate, and the show we saw that night was indiscribable. There were points where we were in pure awe, and during several songs we were drawn to tears. Altogether, it was one of the most emotional concerts I've ever been to, and I feel extra blessed that I got to share the experience with such a dear friend!

Roger Waters 2017

As I mentioned in my rundown of the David Gilmour concert, Pink Floyd is one of my favorite bands. Roger Waters was the bass player and lead vocalist for most of their career. While both Waters and Gilmour still tour playing the music of Pink Floyd, the concerts are completely different experences. One important thing to note is that because of legal reasons surrounding the breakup of Pink Floyd, each member has copyrights to different songs, so Gilmour can perform some songs that Waters can't and vice versa. But, that's not all that separates the two. While Gilmour tries to make his performances almost note-for-note recreations of the Album Version of each song, Waters takes a different approach. Roger Waters considers the thatrical aspect of a concert just as important as the music being played. I've seen Roger Waters several times, but the show in 2017 was by far the best I've seen. He took songs written 40 years earlier and combined them with images of what's going on in the world today. In doing so he showed just how timeless those songs are!

Brian Wilson 2017

I saved Brian Wilson for last, because this show was special to me in so many ways!
